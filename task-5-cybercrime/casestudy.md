## Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

# Type of Cybercrime
UPI/Online Payment Fraud

# What is UPI Payment Fraud?
"UPI payment fraud is a type of cybercrime where fraudsters trick victims into sending money or divulging sensitive payment information in a fraudulent manner."
  
# Actual Case /Fictional Scenario
(Based on real fraud patterns reported in India)

Arjun Sharma, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student from Bhopal, posted his old bicycle for sale on OLX for ₹3,500. Within an hour, he received a call from "Rajesh", who claimed to be an Army officer from 
Pune interested in buying the bicycle immediately.

Rajesh asked Arjun to share his UPI ID for payment via Google Pay. Shortly after, Arjun received a notification asking him to "Accept ₹3,500". Excited, he entered his UPI PIN — only to find ₹3,500 had been 
debited from his account. Rajesh had sent a payment REQUEST, not a payment. By entering his PIN, Arjun had unknowingly authorized sending money to the fraudster. When Arjun called back, the number was already
switched off.

# How it Happened Step by Step
1. Arjun listed his bicycle on a public platform.
2. Fraudster contacted him posing as a genuine buyer.
3. Trust was built by claiming to be an Army officer.
4. A payment REQUEST was sent disguised as a payment.
5. Arjun entered his UPI PIN thinking he was accepting money.
6. ₹3,500 was instantly debited from his account.
7. Fraudster immediately switched off and disappeared.
   
# Who is Usually Targeted
- College students selling items online
- First time UPI users
- People using websites such as OLX, Quikr, Facebook Marketplace, etc.

# Consequences
- Loss of money amounting to ₹3,500.
- Exposure of personal UPI credentials.
- Inability to get money back due to self-authorized transaction.

# What Could Have Been Done Differently
- Never enter UPI PIN for RECEIVE money option.
- Verify the identity of the buyer through a video call.
- Immediately report suspicious contacts to cybercrime.gov.in
